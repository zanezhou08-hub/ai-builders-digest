# 🤖 AI Builders Digest — 2026-07-11

### The MAD Podcast with Matt Turck: Stripe's AI Chief - How AI Agents Will Buy, Sell, and Pay

Emily Cheng, Head of Data and AI at Stripe, provides an in-depth analysis of the current state and future of agentic commerce. The conversation covers how AI agents are transforming commerce infrastructure, the economic implications, and the challenges ahead.

Key insights from Stripe's perspective on agentic commerce:

- **Commerce Spectrum**: Agentic commerce exists on a full spectrum, from fully autonomous agent transactions (MPP) to human-led purchases inside AI surfaces. Most consumer experiences currently hover around Level 2, where people delegate some selection decisions to AI but still choose the exact product.

- **Infrastructure Requirements**: For agentic commerce to work, businesses need to expose product catalogs and prices, consumers need to authorize agents to pay, and agents need to safely execute transactions. This requires new infrastructure beyond traditional commerce systems.

- **Trust and Security**: "Fraudsters have figured out that in AI, you actually don't really need to steal money or credentials. You can just steal tokens." Token theft is described as "the most under discussed topic in all of AI."

- **Token Monetization**: The shift from seat-based to usage-based billing is critical for AI companies. "Many of those businesses have a usage based offering, but it is complementary to what you can think of as like a fixed fee subscription."

- **Stripe's Infrastructure**: Stripe has built the Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP) with partners like OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, and Meta. This allows businesses to expose product catalogs once and opt in to all agents working with that protocol.

- **Economic Impact**: "The number of people solopreneurs who are earning more than a $100,000 a year, has just gone like this since 2022. And now there's in America alone, 5,000,000 people making their living running solo companies."

- **Fraud Prevention**: More than one in six signups at AI companies are multi-account abuse, which represents a significant cost to AI companies due to token consumption.

- **Future Vision**: Cheng predicts that we'll see agents as multifaceted economic actors "buying and selling and provisioning infrastructure and running businesses" within the next 12 months.

Key quote: "This idea of an agent as a micro firm, I think would probably be the most interesting thing to see twelve months from now. And again, I don't think the median firm is going to be... I don't think that's a world to live in, but I think twelve months from now, we could totally see some examples of that that sort of pave the path for the whole thing end to end."
